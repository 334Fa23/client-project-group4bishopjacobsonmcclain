USS Arizona Memorial Tour

The Arizona Memorial Tour is a unique experience that takes visitors to the USS Arizona Memorial in Pearl Harbor, Hawaii. The memorial commemorates the lives lost during the attack on Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941. Visitors begin at the Pearl Harbor Visitor Center, where they can view exhibits and watch a documentary about the attack. They then board a boat to the memorial, which is built over the sunken remains of the USS Arizona. Visitors can pay their respects to the 1,177 sailors and marines who lost their lives and reflect on the impact of the attack in World War II.

Included with the tour:

There are several free museums and exhibits related to Pearl Harbor, Hawai'i. The Pearl Harbor Visitor Center offers free admission to it's museums, including the Road to War exhibit and the Attack Gallery, which features a dramatic multimedia presentation of the attack on Pearl Harbor.

The Pacific Aviation Museum Pearl Harbor also offers free admission to its outdoor displays and exhibits, including aircraft and military artifacts.
