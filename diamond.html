Diamond Head Hike

Diamond Head is a volcanic cone on the Hawaiian island of Oahu that is known for its stunning panoramic views. It is a popular tourist destination and hiking spot, with a steep trail leading to the summit that provides breathtaking views of Waikiki and the Pacific Ocean. The cone was formed over 300,000 years ago and is made of tuff, a type of volcanic ash that has solidified. Diamond Head is named as such because 19th-century British sailors believed they had discovered diamonds on the beach at its base.

Included with the hike:

Complementary scones from Diamond Head Market

Diamond Head Market in Honolulu is famous for its mouth-watering scones. These light, buttery scones are filled with a variety of flavors including blueberry, chocolate chip, and cranberry. The market also offers savory scones made with bacon, cheddar cheese, and jalapeno.

People regularly line up outside the market to taste these delicious treats. The scones are made fresh daily and customers can enjoy them with a cup of coffee or tea. These scones have become a must-try for anyone visiting Oahu, Hawaii.
